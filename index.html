Some key processes

Customer Obsession

While there are several frameworks to manage and build products, having the customer expectations and needs in mind leads always to have the perfect recipe to make a delicious cake.

Technical Program Management / Strategic Direction

Oversee and coordinate the various projects, products, establishing metrics, measuring performance against strategic goals, communicating, managing priorities, and managing other strategic initiatives across an organization is critical for success.

Project Portfolio Management / Prioritization

Effective project selection depends on corporate strategic objectives and goals defined by executive management. The project portfolio is ranked and weighed based on strategic directions, return of investment, shareholders responsibilities, projects timeline, resource availability, company competencies, and financials. In addition, it is important to consider other factors like risks, current market situation, competition, among others to shape the projects prioritization.

BUS Framework

Lay out a solid approach to understand the business objective, define the target users and market, brainstorm about the problems that can be solved, generate solutions for these problems, prioritize them, and make recommendations is key for success.

Working Backwards

Explaining precisely what we hope customers and the media will say when they get their hands on our product/s. Focus on the customer reaction we’re hoping to elicit.

Risk Management / SWOT Analysis

Project Risk Management includes the processes of conducting risk management planning, identification, analysis, response planning, and controlling risks on a project. The main objectives are to increase the likelihood and impact of positive events, and decrease the likelihoold and impact of negative events in the project.

MVP (Exceptional Experience)

Iterating and delivering a great product experience, starting with a Minimum Viable Product, while focusing on doing so at minimal risk and operational cost. Think It, Build it, Ship It, Tweat It.

Financials / Metrics / ROI

Financial management goes well beyond simply planning, capturing, and managing costs on individual projects. It must also address the customer’s need to maintain a balance between the project investment and the expected benefits or returns associated with that project, the potential impact on other projects in the portfolio, and the overall impact on their business results.

Resource Management

Managing and utilizing resources across projects is vital to reach strategic goals of any organization
